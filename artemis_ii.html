Mission overview

Artemis II

A scroll-driven journey from Florida launch to Pacific splashdown, following Orion on a lunar flyby and return.

Launch site Kennedy Space Center
Flight length ~10 days
Recovery Off San Diego
1 · Departure

Launch and parking orbit

Orion rises from Kennedy Space Center and settles into a brief Earth-centered phase before the burn that sends the spacecraft toward the Moon. In the visual model, Earth still dominates the frame to preserve that feeling of proximity and mass.

2 · Outbound

Translunar coast

The spacecraft leaves low Earth space behind and enters a long, quiet cruise. This is the slowest part of the experience, so the path opens up and Orion moves through wider negative space with subtle stars and minimal thrust.

3 · Lunar encounter

Free-return flyby

Near the Moon, the composition tightens and the lunar body grows rapidly to convey arrival. The spacecraft bends around the Moon on a free-return trajectory, using lunar gravity to shape the path back toward Earth.

4 · Homebound

Return to Earth

Earth slowly reclaims the frame as Orion heads home. This section should feel calmer than launch but more directed than the outbound coast, with the path tightening and the destination becoming visually obvious.

5 · Recovery

Re-entry and splashdown

In the final stretch, speed returns. The capsule heats, brightens, then descends to a Pacific splashdown off San Diego, where recovery operations complete the mission arc.
