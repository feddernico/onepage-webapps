Partial Derivatives: Slicing a Surface

We graph the surface z = f(x,y) = sin(x)·cos(y). Choose a slice parallel to the x-axis (fix y = y₀) or parallel to the y-axis (fix x = x₀). The curve in the right plot is exactly the slice; its slope at the red point is the corresponding partial derivative.

Surface view: each square is a point (x,y) colored by height z. The thick line shows the intersection with the vertical plane x = x₀ or y = y₀.
Slice curve: z vs x (if y = y₀ fixed) or z vs y (if x = x₀ fixed). The red dot is at the slice point; its instantaneous slope is the partial derivative.
